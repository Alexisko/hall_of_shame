## 🎿 Emilien Jacquelin — Iconic Races & Moments

---

### 🏆 World Cup & World Championship Highlights

**February 16, 2020 — Pursuit, Worlds Antholz-Anterselva 🥇**
The defining moment of his career, and simultaneously his very first individual World Cup win. Starting 6th from the sprint, he shot a perfect 20/20 and outdueled Johannes Thingnes Bø in a tense tactical last lap, winning by a mere tenth of a second. An absolute classic. For Jacquelin it was the first individual medal at a large biathlon event and his first World Cup victory ever. Martin Fourcade, his mentor, hugged him at the finish line.

**February 2020 — Relay + Mass Start bronze, Worlds Antholz 🥇🥉**
He became the most decorated French athlete at these championships, also winning gold in the men's relay (with Fourcade, Desthieux, Fillon Maillet) and bronze in the mass start. Four medals total at a single Worlds.

**February 14, 2021 — Pursuit, Worlds Pokljuka 🥇**
Il parvient à conserver son titre mondial de la poursuite grâce à un 20/20 au tir bonifié par une grande vitesse d'exécution (1 min 26 s en tout passées sur le tapis), dépassant et distançant ainsi tous ses adversaires en course. Back-to-back world pursuit titles, becoming the discipline's back-to-back champion.

**February 2021 — Sprint bronze, Worlds Pokljuka 🥉**
Added to his already impressive Worlds record alongside the pursuit gold.

**December 19, 2021 — Mass Start, Le Grand-Bornand 🥇**
Sa première victoire ordinaire en Coupe du monde le 19 décembre 2021 au Grand Bornand en réalisant un 19 sur 20 au tir dans la mass-start, après avoir pris les commandes de la course dès le deuxième tour de circuit. He also snatched the yellow bib (overall leader) from Fillon Maillet, creating a French 1-2. His first regular World Cup win — nearly two years after his first victory at Worlds.

**February 2023 — Men's Relay gold, Worlds Oberhof 🥇**
His third World Championship relay gold. The individual season was poor and he ended it early, citing burnout ("the tank is empty"), but this team gold was a bright spot.

---

### 🏅 Olympic Games

**Beijing 2022 — Mixed Relay silver 🥈**
With Fillon Maillet, Chevalier-Bouchet, and Julia Simon. His first Olympic medal.

**Beijing 2022 — Men's Relay silver 🥈**
With Fillon Maillet, Desthieux, and Fabien Claude. A double silver to remember, even if individual medals eluded him.

**Milan-Cortina 2026 — Pursuit bronze 🥉**
Third in the biathlon pursuit at the Milan/Cortina 2026 Olympic Games, Emilien Jacquelin won his first career individual Olympic medal. Emotional finish — he was thinking of Marco Pantani, who passed away 22 years prior to the race day. He had worn Pantani's pirate earring as a tribute throughout the Games.

**Milan-Cortina 2026 — Men's Relay gold 🥇**
Placé en deuxième relayeur du quatuor français, il est lancé par Fabien Claude avec 50 secondes de retard sur la tête de course, il remonte et passe le relais à Quentin Fillon Maillet en tête. A stunning relay leg — coming back from 50 seconds behind to hand over in first place. France wins Olympic gold.

---

### 📌 Extra Notable Moment

**2025 Worlds Lenzerheide — Mixed Relay gold 🥇**
Another World Championship relay gold, adding to his already remarkable team record.

---

**In summary:** Jacquelin holds 5 World Championship gold medals (2× pursuit individual, 3× relay), 3 Olympic medals (2× silver in 2022, 1× bronze + 1× gold in 2026), and is one of the most emotionally expressive and dramatic racers in biathlon history.
